Frequently Asked Questions

What is ARIMA forecasting?
ARIMA (AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average) is a statistical method used to predict future values based on past patterns in your data. It analyzes historical husked coconut prices to forecast what prices might be in the coming days.
What do the parameters (p, d, q) mean?
p (AR Order) - How many previous days' prices affect today's price. If p=2, today depends on the last 2 days.

d (Differencing) - How many times we remove trends from the data. Usually 1 is enough to make prices stable.

q (MA Order) - How many previous prediction errors are used. Helps smooth out random fluctuations.
Example: ARIMA(1,1,1) means "use yesterday's price, remove trends once, and consider yesterday's error"
What are Lower Bound and Upper Bound?
These show the confidence interval - a range where the actual price is likely to fall:
  • Forecast Price: The most likely predicted price
  • Lower Bound: The pessimistic scenario (worst case)
  • Upper Bound: The optimistic scenario (best case)
Forecast: ₱15.00 | Lower: ₱13.50 | Upper: ₱16.50
Meaning: "We're 95% confident the price will be between ₱13.50 - ₱16.50"
Should I use manual parameters or auto-select?
Auto-Select (Recommended for beginners): The system tests multiple parameter combinations and picks the best one based on statistical quality (AIC score).

Manual Parameters: Use if you understand ARIMA or want to experiment with specific values. Start with (1,1,1) as a baseline.
What is AIC and BIC in the results?
AIC (Akaike Information Criterion) and BIC (Bayesian Information Criterion) measure how good the model is.

Lower values = Better model
These help compare different ARIMA models. The auto-select feature uses AIC to find the best parameters for your data.
How accurate is the forecast?
Forecast accuracy depends on:
  • Data Quality: More historical transactions = better predictions
  • Market Stability: Stable prices are easier to predict than volatile ones
  • Time Horizon: Near-term forecasts (7-14 days) are more reliable than long-term (60-90 days)
The confidence interval (shaded area) shows uncertainty. A wider interval means less certainty.
How can I use this forecast for my business?
Practical Applications:
  • Buying Decisions: If prices are forecasted to rise, consider buying more stock now
  • Selling Strategy: If prices are forecasted to drop, you might sell sooner
  • Budget Planning: Use the forecast to estimate future costs and revenues
  • Risk Management: The lower bound helps you prepare for worst-case scenarios
Why do I need at least 10 data points?
ARIMA needs enough historical data to identify patterns and trends. With fewer than 10 transactions, the model cannot reliably predict future prices. More data = Better forecasts. Try to accumulate more transaction history for improved predictions.
